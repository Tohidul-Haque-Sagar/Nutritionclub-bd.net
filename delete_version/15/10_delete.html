About Us

The Bangladesh Institute of ICT in Development (BIID) Foundation, a social business, introduced the Nutrition Club (NC) platform back in 2015 to engage and benefit adolescents and youth in achieving healthy body and mind, with the skills to make informed decisions and lead healthy lifestyles.

What is Nutrition Club?

Ignite the Spark of Young People!

The Nutrition Club provides a platform for adolescents and youth to build capacity, disseminate knowledge, and raise awareness about nutrition. Being part of the Nutrition Club is more than just nutrition and health - it's a lifestyle.

Having the confidence and skills to lead healthy lifestyles not only improves overall well-being; it enables them to tackle life's challenges today and become leaders in their lives, careers, and communities as they grow into responsible adulthood.

Our Causes

Nourishing the Future

Nutrition Club is mandated to build leadership skills and create opportunities for young people to improve nutrition for all. NC envisions creating a well-nourished society to support national and global efforts to end malnutrition across Bangladesh and globally.

Nutrition Club Programs Engage Youth to Lead in Nutrition

Nutrition Club programs are designed targeting adolescents and youths to learn by doing. Members engage in a wide range of projects focusing on nutrition which covers health, hygiene, agriculture, skill development, creative works, environment, and civic engagement under the direct guidance of the Mentors at respective institutions.

1. Building Awareness, Networking & Engaging

Organizing campaigns, competitions, and community events to spread nutrition awareness and build networks among youth volunteers across urban and rural communities.

Community Outreach
Networking

2. Developing Team Building, Leadership & Soft Skills

Orientation sessions, boot camps, innovation labs, and design camps to develop leadership and soft skills for adolescents and youth.

Leadership
Skill Building

3. Dissemination of Nutrition Knowledge

Nutrition fairs, courtyard meetings, peer-to-peer learning, and online platform engagement to spread nutrition information, education and communication (IEC).

Knowledge Sharing
IEC

4. Organize and participate in National & International Events

Participating in nutrition weeks, international days, olympiads, and global awareness campaigns to address national and global development agenda.

Global Impact
SDG Goals

e-Learning: e-Pushti

Making ICT Works for Nutrition

An online learning platform (www.e-pushti.net) has been developed and designed to create opportunities for adolescents and young people to learn about nutrition, healthy lifestyles, and various social issues that directly and indirectly affect physical and mental health, and raise awareness.

It has a set of activities to enhance personal and social skills, leadership abilities, through nutrition-related activities. This will widen the opportunity for all segments of society to have inclusive access to online courses, as well as facilitate the young people.

Changemakers in Action!

Nutrition Clubs organize a comprehensive set of activities under different Programs to engage youth as influential agents of change for improved nutrition and healthy lifestyles.

1. Building Awareness, Networking & Engaging

  • Organize Campaigns on nutrition and healthy lifestyle
  • Host competitions including Painting, Recipe, Debate, Music, Games etc.
  • Establish MHM (Menstrual Health Management) Corner
  • Organize plastic free campus, initiative naming Clean Campus: Green Campus
  • Cooking Demonstration
  • Peer-to-peer learning
  • Community events, etc.

2. Developing Team Building, Leadership & Soft Skills

  • Orientation Sessions
    (Briefing on Nutrition Club, Operational Modality, Roles of NC Executive Committee, Preparation of Plan of Action)
  • Boot Camp
  • Innovation Lab
  • Design Camp
  • Thematic Discussion Session (WASH, Healthy Diet, Safe Food, MHM etc.)
  • Day Observance and Celebrations etc.
  • Exchange programs

3. Dissemination
of Nutrition Knowledge

  • Nutrition Fair
  • Courtyard Meeting
  • Peer-to-Peer learning
  • Online platform
    e-Pushti.net
  • SBCC Materials
    (Social and Behavior Change Communication)
  • School Nutrition Sessions
  • Health and Nutrition Camps
  • Nutri Garden

4. National & International Events and Day Celebrations

  • National Nutrition Week
  • National and International Girl Child Day
  • International Nutrition Olympiad (INO)
  • World Food Day
  • 16 Days Activism etc.

🎁 BIID Foundation Launched Nutrition Club Store

BIID Foundation launched the Nutrition Club Store, an initiative to provide a centralized source of supplies NC materials. This aimed to foster a sense of belonging and increase public awareness of the Nutrition Club initiative and its activities by offering items featuring the NC emblem.

It was also intended to support the Nutrition Clubs to mobilize resources to meet their needs to conduct activities and promote the NC locally.
